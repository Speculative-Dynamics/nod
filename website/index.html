No signal? No problem.

Listens even when you
can't connect.

On a plane. In a tunnel. On a trail with one bar. Nod doesn't care — the entire conversation runs on the same chip that renders this message. No network required.

Apple Intelligence is ready the moment you install the app. If you switch to an open-weights engine in settings, the weights download once, then they're yours forever. After that, Nod is as offline as your camera.

Your call.

Pick the mind
that listens.

Nod ships with four on-device engines. Apple's own model is the default, so Nod works the second you open it — no download, nothing to configure.

Prefer open weights? Switch to Qwen 3 Instruct 2507, Qwen 3.5 4B, or Gemma 4 E2B in settings. Nod pulls the weights once over Wi-Fi, then runs them entirely on your phone. No accounts, no API keys, no tokens to budget.

Nod remembers.

What matters to you,
on your phone.

After each conversation, Nod quietly picks up the details that seem to matter — the people in your life, the things you're working on, the situations you keep coming back to. It writes them down in a list you can actually see.

Any entry you want gone, you swipe away. Want to wipe the whole thing? Tap Start fresh. It's all local — no profile builds up in a data center somewhere, because there is no data center.

Speak, don't type.

Or just talk.

Tap the mic and start speaking. A warm orange glow hugs the edge of the screen while Nod listens. It commits when you pause — no tap required.

Transcription runs on-device using iOS 26's SpeechAnalyzer. What you say never leaves the phone, not even for speech-to-text.

Tune it. Lock it.

Make it yours.
Keep it yours.

Two quiet pickers and a free-form field let you shape how Nod talks to you — shorter or deeper, pure listening or a little reflection. Add a line about yourself and Nod tucks it into every response.

Turn on Require Face ID and the whole app sits behind biometric auth. Close it, open it, the conversation waits for you — nobody else.

Keep it private

Privacy is architecture.

Runs entirely
on your phone.

Nod has no backend. There's no server to leak, no account to delete, no API key to revoke. Every word you type stays in a local database on the device, read only by a model running on the same chip. iCloud backup for the conversation is off by default unless you turn it on.

We can't see what you write. Not because we promise — because Nod has no backend receiving your conversation.

  • Default engine Apple FoundationModels Apple Intelligence's on-device LLM. Runs the second you open the app — no download, no network.
  • Alternative engines Qwen 3, Qwen 3.5, Gemma 4 Open-weights models via MLX Swift. Switch in settings — Nod downloads the selected model once (2–3 GB, Wi-Fi by default), then runs on-device forever.
  • Memory On-device fact extraction Apple FoundationModels extracts structured memories from each turn. Browse them in the sidebar; delete any one of them, or all of them.
  • Storage Local SQLite GRDB.swift, encrypted at rest by iOS. Tap "Start fresh" and it's gone. iCloud backup is optional and off by default.
  • Analytics None No telemetry, no tracking pixels, no third-party SDKs.
